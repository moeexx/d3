The relationship between GDP and Energy consumption

Introduction

This project aims to explore the relationship between GDP and energy consumption from multiple perspective. I select 30 typical countries from different development levels around the world such as the developed level, the developing level and even those in the war. From previous statistics calculation, I verify my assumption that GDP and energy consumption has a positive relationship. Thus, based on this rule, can you find something different such as those countries which is not corresponded with this trend and those years when part of GDP data got disappear?

Here are my conclusion:



Missing value

● From 1991 to 2011, the gulf war and the Iraq war broke out successively in Iraq. Due to the war, the GDP statistics may be interrupted, so the data on this aspect is not available.
● The Russian Federation was established in 1990 so that there was not any data available before 1990.

Irregular counties

● The energy consumption per capita of US is similar to that of Trinidad and Tobago, but whom GDP per capita is three times higher than that of Trinidad and Tobago, while Iceland with a similar GDP per capita of the US, has much more energy consumption.
● Japan, where the energy consumption per capita is only 40% of Kuwait's, has yet higher GDP than Kuwait's.

Irregular time period

● In 1980, because of oil price control the organization of the petroleum exporting countries (OPEC), the second oil crisis broke out. As a result, GDP per capita in Kuwait, Qatar and other oil-producing countries exploded during this period.
● In 1995, the world trade organization (WTO) was formally established. Affected by economic globalization, the global trade of each country began to increase GDP per capita markedly.
● In 1998, affected by the Asian financial crisis, the GDP per capita of the countries in the Asia-pacific dropped significantly. Due to the impact of economic globalization, some European and American countries were also affected by the Asian financial crisis.
● In 2009, the GDP of most countries in the world affected by the global economic crisis, dropping sharply.

Map chart

From the geographical perspective, countries with higher GDP (per capita) are concentrated in North America, Europe and Oceania. Latin America, Africa and Asia have lower GDP (per capita).
Countries with high energy consumption(per capita) are distributed in North America, Western Europe and northern Europe, while GDP(per capita) in Africa, Latin America and the eastern half of Eurasia is low. This is roughly in line with the distribution of GDP per capita.

Iceland, Tobago and Kuwait saw their energy consumption head rise and fall in line with the tendency of GDP.
